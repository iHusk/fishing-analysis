Concept 5: Smart-Default Summary + Two-Tap Confirm

Paradigm: a catch receipt, not a form; the app assumes the trip and Tyler defaults are right until corrected.

How it works: glance the receipt, tap only changed tiles, then tap Looks right and Log catch; the same two actions map cleanly to a future BLE remote.

No-scroll strategy: one hero value, compact two-column correction tiles, and one full-width GPS/notes row keep every field inside 390x844.

Outside-box angle: confirmation is the primary control surface, while editing is exception handling through visible value chips.

Risks: stale defaults could be accepted too quickly, so the default source, GPS lock, and editable chips stay visible at all times.
